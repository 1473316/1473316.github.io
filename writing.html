The Scarecrow

At the end of the day she walked
To the edge of her garden
Surrounded by a fence, picket
White as snow if you looked on from afar but
Here, underneath her fingers, she could
See its bumps and birthmarks
Pick at the paint that was peeling away

She stood at the edge of her garden
And she watched as the sun set low
Forwent the mourning of its passing.
The sky falling dark and the
Stars, one by one turning on blinking lights
Tiny peepholes past a blanket of night

The House behind her is dark
Nothing shines from its windows
Its door hangs on hinges, battered and
Rotten. A piano sing somewhere inside
A lullaby, soft and just a half beat behind
The drum of the heart

Long, the music will play
Fingers harden and shoulders sag
The moon has appeared in the sky, a gaping hole
A glowing eye, she stares up at it
Standing at the edge of her garden
She listens until the final note.

And the House, like made of sand
Like made of sticks and stones, collapses
Brick by brick it crumbles, dust rises
And dances in the air, stings her eyes.

Now there is a cross to her left
Eight feet high and made from peeling,
Rotten wood. Fences. Doors.

She climbs this cross, woe upon her
Slivers slice her hands and kiss her skin
Dried hay and holey clothes is all it has become
And she hangs there, a scarecrow
As night settles in, she hangs there
At the edge of her garden
Looking up with button eyes
Through tiny peepholes in the sky

The Heart of the Emperor

The Heart of the Emperor is a short fantasy story written by Dominique Kalunga. Published on Vocal as part of their Barn Owl Challenege, it is a story about nature, and self identity.

The Heart of the Emperor

Green

Tonight, there are pickled branches wrung tight:
wash cloths pulled of their last pearl.
And swirls carved deep in nature’s flesh
used to hold a secret youth.
Those knobby knuckles and reaching limbs, undead
Shivering leaves and prickling thorns they bend
around his throne.
And inside the boy is green.

Poisoned bark leaks beneath skin
Curdle untouched blood
And darkens the apples beneath his checks
And still, the boy is green

Branches whittled sharp by wind and wishes
Creep above the head rest and
call themselves a crown
Spotted nails for his brow
Still, the boy is green

Scabs of moss grow from his ears
Be still and be sound
Tuck him in under a blanket of black
Heavy. Maggot holed.
Still, the boy is green

Ripe, the fruits hang from its arms
Swollen by crawling brown-bodied creepers
Reeking of rot as they swing by his head
Slipping through cracks when he takes a bite
Still, the boy is green

And when the ax comes
Whistling it’s tune as it slices through air
Pickled branches. Poisoned bark.
When the carcass screams
Revealing yellow teeth and rattling lungs
When the throne cracks

Still, the boy is green
Holding fast, holding tight
Still, the boy is green
Clinging to brittle bones
Still, the boy is green
Face streaked by the sticky sap
That fills his veins

A note rings
A snap, and arms give out
The corpse and the boy
Come crashing down

Broken on a forest floor
Twisted limbs, maple blood
Crown upon his head
Mangled branches his pillow
His cage, his bed.
Pearl eyes bright and blind

The ax whistles away

And still, even now
Even now.
The boy is green
